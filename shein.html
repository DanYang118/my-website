Shein Redesign

Background

Analysis of Shein’s product pages, which users found cluttered and confusing. Collaborated in a 4-person team

The Goal

Simplify navigation and improve clarity in promotions and interactions.

Solution

Delivered research insights, IA improvements, wireframes, and a high-fidelity prototype of Shein’s PDP redesign. The new design made product browsing more intuitive, reduced user frustration, and aligned better with Shein’s goal of increasing conversion rates through a smoother shopping flow.

Try this prototype

Core Competencies Applied

Heuristic Evaluation
Benchmarked the existing interface against Jakob Nielsen’s 10 usability heuristics to identify critical friction points in navigation and clarity.
Information Architecture
Restructured the product information hierarchy to prioritize essential buying criteria and reduce visual noise.
Tree Testing
Validated the findability of specific product attributes within the new site structure to ensure a logical navigation flow.
Card Sorting
Organized product features and marketing elements based on user mental models to create more intuitive content groupings.
User Journey Mapping
Visualized the end-to-end browsing experience to identify emotional pain points during the transition from discovery to cart.
Wireframing
Developed low-to-mid fidelity structural blueprints to test the new layout without the distraction of visual styling.
Interactive Prototyping
Built a high-fidelity Figma prototype to demonstrate the seamless transition and improved interaction logic of the redesigned page.
