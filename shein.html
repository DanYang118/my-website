Shein Product Detail Page Redesign

Try this prototype

Background

Analysis of Shein’s product pages, which users found cluttered and confusing. Collaborated in a 4-person team

Goal

Simplify navigation and improve clarity in promotions and interactions.

Solution

Delivered research insights, IA improvements, wireframes, and a high-fidelity prototype of Shein’s PDP redesign. The new design made product browsing more intuitive, reduced user frustration, and aligned better with Shein’s goal of increasing conversion rates through a smoother shopping flow.

Design Process

  • Heuristic Evaluation: Evaluated the PDP against usability heuristics; identified duplicate navigation bars, unclear category hierarchy, and distracting scrolling banners.
  • Problem Investigation: Tree testing and card sorting showed key usability issues in Shein’s product page, including unclear CTAs, misclassification content, and hidden product details. These misalignments with user mental models caused confusion and slowed decision-making.
  • User Research: Conducted peer walkthroughs and surveys to validate pain points.
  • IA-focused methods: including card sorting and tree testing, to evaluate and improve Shein’s product page navigation.
  • Empathy Map: Visualized user thoughts/feelings during the shopping journey to highlight frustration points.
  • Wireframes (Hana): Created low-fidelity layouts to explore simpler navigation and clearer product hierarchy.
  • Iterations: Refined layout and deal presentation based on test feedback.
  • High-Fidelity Mockups (Me & Hana): Designed high-fidelity mockups to validate the new design and user flow.
  • Prototype (Me & Hana): Built an interactive prototype to demonstrate the new design and user flow.
