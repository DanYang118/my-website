Senior chore share design

Try this prototype

Background

Seniors living alone face difficulties with chores, safety, and social connectedness. Collaborated in a 4-person team

Goal

Improve independence by designing a simple tool for requesting help and support.

Solution

A wall-mounted SOS panel that allows seniors to:

  • Make emergency calls with a single large button.
  • Request help for daily chores via simple icons or voice.
  • Coordinate with caregivers through a clear, easy-to-read calendar.

Design Process

  • User Research: Conducted surveys (20+ valid responses) and interviews to uncover seniors’ challenges in household tasks, safety, and social isolation.
  • Data Analysis: Visualized findings (household task reliance, loneliness, connectedness) to highlight key needs.
  • Empathy Map: Captured seniors’ thoughts, feelings, and behaviours to deepen understanding.
  • Feedback Categorization: Grouped constructive and destructive feedback to identify priority issues.
  • Ideation & Wireframes: Sketched and tested early layouts for a wall-mounted SOS panel.
  • High-Fidelity Mockups: Designed final screens in Figma with large emergency button, clear task requests, and simple navigation.
  • Prototype (Jeremy): Built an interactive prototype to simulate emergency calls and task requests.
  • Usability Testing: Tested with peers, refined button size, task flow, and navigation clarity based on feedback.
